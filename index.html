My Marketing Journey

How I was led here.

🎵
Chapter 01
Performance & Music
I started learning both piano and vocal performance at age 5. Over the years, I competed in singing competitions, performed on stage, and was part of creative music projects, including being featured in a government-promoted music video.

What performing gave me was not just technique. It gave me a real sense of how to hold an audience's attention, how to communicate something so that it lands, not just reaches. That instinct toward expression and audience connection has stayed with me.
ExpressionAudience ConnectionStorytellingCreativity
🧑‍🏫
Chapter 02
Teaching & Mentorship
Teaching has been a constant throughout my life, starting in high school when I volunteered in remote rural villages to teach children with limited access to education. Later, I taught at one of Vancouver's largest Chinese-language schools, ran summer camps, mentored first-year students through UBC Sauder's Commerce Mentorship Program, and continued volunteering in different community settings.

Across all of it, I've worked with 100+ students. The biggest thing I took from that is how much it matters to understand who you're talking to before you try to reach them. That empathy and audience-first thinking is exactly what good marketing requires too.
EmpathyAudience UnderstandingLeadershipCommunication
🍢
Chapter 03
Building Things
My family owns a hotpot restaurant, and from high school onwards, I'd sit down with my dad to think through how to bring in more customers. One idea I worked on was a Rush Hour Package, a promotion for the restaurant's slow periods. After we launched it, sales during those hours went up by roughly 20%. That was the first time I really felt what it's like to solve a real problem with a marketing idea and see it work.

That curiosity kept going. When my internship needed a social media calendar, I built Aura Studio, an AI-powered content assistant that gradually grew into something with many more features. I also built this website. I still think of myself as someone who's learning, but I like figuring things out by doing them.
Problem SolvingCuriosityMarketing ThinkingBuilding
📣
Chapter 04
Marketing Experiences
Working at Yvonne's Besties, The Borgen Project, and Project Mind Foundation, alongside building Aura Studio, gave me my first real look at what marketing involves day to day: figuring out who you're talking to, what they care about, and how to create something that actually reaches them.

Brand positioning, content strategy, multi-channel campaigns, consumer insights — these stopped being textbook concepts and started feeling like real problems to work through. I'm still learning, but these experiences gave me enough of a foundation to know this is the direction I want to keep going.
Brand PositioningContent StrategyConsumer InsightsAI Tools
Every chapter in this journey gave me something. Performing taught me how to read a room. Teaching taught me how to understand people before trying to reach them. Building things taught me that the best way to learn is to start. When I found marketing, it felt like all of those pieces finally had somewhere to go. That is why I keep going.
